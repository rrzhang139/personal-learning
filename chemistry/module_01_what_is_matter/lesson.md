# Module 1: What is Matter?

> This is our very first lesson. No prerequisites — we start from zero.

---

## 1.1 What is Matter?

**Everything you can touch, see, or feel is made of matter.**

Matter is anything that:
1. Has **mass** (it has "stuff" in it)
2. Takes up **space** (it occupies volume)

```
Examples of matter:          NOT matter:
  - A rock                     - Light
  - Water                      - Sound
  - Air (yes, air!)            - Heat
  - Your body                  - An idea
```

**Key insight:** Even things you can't see (like air) are matter — they have mass and take up space. You can feel wind push against you. That's air (matter) in motion.

---

## 1.2 What is Matter Made Of?

If you zoom in on any piece of matter — say, a gold ring — you'd see this:

```
Gold Ring (zoom in...)

  Visible:     [=====ring=====]

  Microscope:  . . . . . . . .
               . . . . . . . .    <-- grains of gold
               . . . . . . . .

  Atomic:      (Au) (Au) (Au)
               (Au) (Au) (Au)     <-- individual gold atoms
               (Au) (Au) (Au)
```

Everything is made of **atoms** — incredibly tiny particles. An atom is so small that about 10 million of them lined up side by side would span 1 millimeter.

---

## 1.3 Elements

An **element** is a substance made of only ONE type of atom.

- Gold is an element — it's made entirely of gold atoms (Au)
- Oxygen is an element — it's made entirely of oxygen atoms (O)
- Iron is an element — it's made entirely of iron atoms (Fe)

There are about **118 known elements**. Everything in the universe is built from combinations of these 118 building blocks.

Think of elements like **LEGO brick types**. You have ~118 different brick shapes, and everything in the universe is built by snapping them together.

```
  Element = one type of brick

  [RED] [RED] [RED]  = pure element (e.g., a bar of pure iron)

  [RED] [BLUE] [RED] = NOT a pure element (it's a compound — more on this soon)
```

## 1.4 States of Matter

Matter exists in three common states (we'll learn about others later):

```
  SOLID              LIQUID              GAS
  +---------+       +---------+        +---------+
  | o o o o |       | o  o o  |        | o       |
  | o o o o |       |  o  o   |        |      o  |
  | o o o o |       | o o  o  |        |  o      |
  | o o o o |       |  o o  o |        |    o    |
  +---------+       +---------+        +---------+
  Particles:        Particles:         Particles:
  - Fixed in place  - Slide around     - Fly freely
  - Vibrate only    - Stay close-ish   - Far apart
  - Definite shape  - Take shape of    - Fill entire
  - Definite volume   container          container
                    - Definite volume  - No fixed volume
```

**What determines the state?** Energy (temperature).
- Add energy (heat) → particles move faster → solid melts to liquid → liquid boils to gas
- Remove energy (cool) → particles slow down → gas condenses to liquid → liquid freezes to solid

```
  SOLID  --heat-->  LIQUID  --heat-->  GAS
         <--cool--          <--cool--
```

## 1.5 Pure Substances vs. Mixtures

```
                        MATTER
                          |
              +-----------+-----------+
              |                       |
       PURE SUBSTANCE             MIXTURE
              |                       |
       +------+------+        +------+------+
       |             |        |             |
    ELEMENT      COMPOUND  HOMOGENEOUS  HETEROGENEOUS
   (one type    (two+ types  (uniform     (not uniform)
    of atom)    chemically    throughout)
                 bonded)
```

**Pure substance:** Same composition throughout, has fixed properties
- **Element:** Only one type of atom (gold, oxygen, carbon)
- **Compound:** Two or more elements chemically bonded in fixed ratios (water = H2O, always 2 hydrogen + 1 oxygen)

**Mixture:** Two or more substances physically combined (NOT chemically bonded)
- **Homogeneous:** Uniform — looks the same throughout (salt water, air)
- **Heterogeneous:** Not uniform — you can see different parts (salad, sand + rocks)

**Key difference:** Compounds can only be separated by chemical reactions. Mixtures can be separated by physical methods (filtering, evaporating, etc.)

## Quick Check

Try to answer these before looking at the answers:

1. Is light matter? Why or why not?
2. What is the difference between an element and a compound?
3. If you dissolve sugar in water, is that a mixture or a compound?
4. Ice, water, and steam are all the same element/compound — what's different?

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. No — light has no mass and doesn't take up space
2. An element is made of one type of atom; a compound is two or more types chemically bonded
3. A mixture (homogeneous) — sugar and water aren't chemically bonded, you can boil off the water to get sugar back
4. They're all H2O (water). The state of matter is different — solid, liquid, gas — because of different energy levels

</details>
