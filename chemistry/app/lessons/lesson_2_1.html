Quick Recap

Before we go inside the atom, let's remember what we know:

From Module 1: Everything is made of matter (mass + volume). Matter is made of atoms. A substance with one type of atom is an element (118 total). Atoms can be in three states — solid, liquid, gas — depending on their energy.

We've been treating atoms like tiny solid balls. But what's inside an atom?

2.1 Inside the Atom

An atom is NOT a solid ball. It has internal structure — like a tiny solar system:

At the center: the nucleus (protons + neutrons). Orbiting far outside: electrons.

The Insane Scale

If the atom were the size of a football stadium, the nucleus would be a marble at center field. The electrons buzz around in the upper seats. Most of the atom is empty space.

Distilled into Math

Atomic Number (what element it is):
Z = number of protons
Mass Number (total nuclear particles):
A = Z + N
Number of neutrons:
N = A - Z
SymbolNameWhat it means
ZAtomic numberNumber of protons — defines the element
AMass numberProtons + neutrons — total mass of nucleus
NNeutron numberNeutrons only — varies between isotopes
Why Z matters most: Change the number of protons → you change the element. 6 protons is ALWAYS carbon. 79 protons is ALWAYS gold. No exceptions.

Why N can vary: Same element can have different numbers of neutrons. Carbon-12 has 6 neutrons, Carbon-14 has 8. Same element, different isotope. (More on this in 2.3!)
