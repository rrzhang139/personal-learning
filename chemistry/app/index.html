Quick Recap

Before we dive into states of matter, let's remember what we've learned:

From lessons 1.1–1.3: Matter is anything with mass and volume. All matter is made of tiny particles called atoms. A substance made of just one type of atom is an element. There are 118 known elements — the building blocks of everything.

Now the big question: why does water sometimes flow, sometimes freeze into a block, and sometimes float away as steam? It's the same atoms — so what's different?

Why Does Temperature Change the State?

Temperature is really a measure of kinetic energy — how fast the particles are moving.

Low energy (cold): Particles barely move. They're held in place by attractive forces between them → SOLID

Medium energy (warm): Particles have enough energy to break free of their fixed positions, but not enough to fly apart → LIQUID

High energy (hot): Particles have so much energy they completely overcome the attractive forces and fly freely → GAS
